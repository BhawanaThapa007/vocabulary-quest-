Nurture
/NUR-cher/
Verb
To care for and encourage the growth of someone.
"Good parents nurture their children with love and patience."
Compassion
/kum-PASH-un/
Noun
Sympathy and concern for someone who is suffering.
"She showed great compassion by helping her injured classmate."
Cherish
/CHER-ish/
Verb
To hold someone or something very dear to your heart.
"We cherish every moment we spend with our grandparents."
Sibling
/SIB-ling/
Noun
A brother or sister.
"My sibling and I share a bedroom and read together every night."
Ancestor
/AN-ses-ter/
Noun
A person in your family who lived a very long time ago.
"Our ancestors from Nepal built beautiful temples long ago."
Bond
/BOND/
Noun
A strong feeling of connection between people.
"The bond between twins is often very special and very deep."
Respect
/reh-SPEKT/
Noun/Verb
To show deep admiration and honour for someone.
"Children should always show respect to their elders and teachers."
Generous
/JEN-er-us/
Adjective
Willing to give and share freely with others.
"His generous grandmother always brought gifts when she visited."

📝 Unit 2 Story — Family & Relations
Rina had a very strong bond with her sibling Raj. Their parents always nurtured them with love, food, and good education every day. Rina deeply cherished the stories her grandmother told about their ancestors who came from the hills so long ago. Her grandmother was also extremely generous — she always shared her food and time with everyone around her. One day Raj fell and hurt his knee badly. Rina showed great compassion by helping clean his wound and staying by his side all day. She also showed great respect by listening carefully to everything her parents said.
